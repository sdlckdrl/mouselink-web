Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

OneMouse connects a Windows PC, an Android device, and paired Windows PCs in the user's local environment. This policy explains what information is processed and how ads and billing SDKs are used.

Effective date: May 10, 2026

01

Core principle

OneMouse is built around local communication between the user's PC, Android device, and paired PCs. OneMouse does not operate a separate account server that stores user files, clipboard content, or input content.

02

Information processed

  • Device information: Android device ID, device name, PC name, PC device ID, or local network address
  • Connection information: pairing state, session key, selected default PC, and last connection time
  • Placement information: Android screen size, orientation, and phone position around the PC monitor
  • Transfer information: files, text, and clipboard content explicitly sent by the user
  • Billing information: whether Pro was purchased or restored. Google Play handles payment processing.
  • Advertising signals: Google Mobile Ads SDK may process advertising identifiers and related signals for Free mode ads.

03

Accessibility permission

The Android Accessibility service is used to apply mouse and keyboard input sent from the PC to the Android screen. OneMouse does not use Accessibility for ad tracking, screen content collection, password collection, or remote server transmission.

04

Files and clipboard

Files and clipboard content move only when the user sends them or enables the relevant sync feature. They are transferred between the user's own PC, Android device, and paired PCs and are not uploaded to OneMouse servers.

05

Ads and billing

Free mode banner ads use Google Mobile Ads SDK. Pro purchases and purchase restoration use Google Play Billing. Google's own terms and privacy policies may apply to those services.

Updated Guide

From your first connection to everyday features, step by step.

OneMouse helps you control Android and other Windows PCs with your PC mouse and keyboard. This guide covers only what you need right after installing: the connection order, where to look in the app, file transfer, mobile screen remote control, shortcuts, and the flow for connecting another PC.

Quick Start

For your first connection, just follow this order.

There is no need to touch complex network settings yourself. Open the PC app, find the PC from Android, then confirm with the QR or 6-digit code.

1
Run the PC app

When you open OneMouse on Windows, the PC screen shows a 6-digit code and a QR code. The latest Setup file connects automatically from the download page.

2
Turn on the Accessibility permission in Android

To control Android with your PC mouse and keyboard, the OneMouse Accessibility service is required. This is effectively the one item you should treat as mandatory during first setup.

3
Search for the PC

If the PC and Android are on the same Wi-Fi or under the same router, the app can find the PC. On Android 13 and above, the nearby Wi-Fi devices permission may be requested only at this moment.

4
Pair with the QR or 6-digit code

Scan the QR on the PC screen or enter the 6-digit code. Only after this step can you use the input, file, and clipboard channels.

5
Place the phone on a PC edge

Place Android on the left, right, top, or bottom of your monitor, wherever you will actually move the cursor across. This placement becomes the cursor entry direction.

6
Use input, transfer, and mobile screen remote control

Push the mouse in the placement direction to move to Android or a Sub PC. Send PC files to the Quick Drop zone, or in PC-to-PC, hold a file and drag it across the screen edge to drop it on the target PC. Use the remote control icon in Device management to open the mobile screen remote control window, and press Print Screen or Ctrl + Alt + S to bring a mobile screenshot up in the PC's default viewer.

Bluetooth

You can also connect over Bluetooth, without a router.

Even without the same Wi-Fi, or when the internet is down, Bluetooth connects your PC to Android — and to other PCs — directly so you can use mouse and keyboard input, clipboard, and file transfer. No router or internet connection is required.

When To Use

Handy in these cases

  • Meeting rooms, on the go, or in a car, where you can't put both on the same Wi-Fi.
  • When the PC isn't discoverable because of guest Wi-Fi or a firewall.
  • When you want a light setup mainly for keyboard input and clipboard.

Order

Set up Bluetooth in this order.

Turn on Bluetooth mode in Android first, pair once in Windows, and the PC app connects automatically. You don't need to press a connect button on the PC.

1
Turn on Bluetooth mode on the Android Settings tab

Turn on Bluetooth mode on the Settings tab in the OneMouse app, and Android waits for the PC to connect.

2
Pair the phone in Windows Bluetooth settings

Add the phone in Windows Bluetooth & devices settings and pair it once. That's all there is to it. You only need to pair the first time.

3
The PC app connects automatically

Once pairing is done, the PC app finds the Bluetooth device and connects automatically. It appears as a Bluetooth device in the Device management list.

4
Use input, clipboard, and file transfer

Just like a Wi-Fi connection, you can use mouse and keyboard input, two-way clipboard, and file transfer.

See Bluetooth screens and demos Device list, PC-keyboard typing, and file drag shown in short clips.

Notes

Good to know

  • Turn Bluetooth on or off with the toggle on the Settings tab; it works independently of your Wi-Fi connection. When it's on, it connects automatically once the phone is ready, and if no phone is found it keeps retrying gently at gradually longer intervals.
  • You can connect to another PC over Bluetooth too. Set one as the Main PC and the other as the Sub PC to pass mouse and keyboard, share the clipboard, and drag files across the screen edge in both directions (send and pull back).
  • You can connect several Bluetooth devices at once. Turn each one on or off with its toggle in the Bluetooth list under Device management.
  • Bluetooth is slower than Wi-Fi. It suits mouse and keyboard input, clipboard, and small files; for large files, Wi-Fi is recommended.
  • Mobile screen remote control is a bandwidth-heavy feature, so it's not recommended over Bluetooth.
  • If the connection doesn't appear, check that the phone is paired in Windows and that Bluetooth mode is on in Android.

Remote Control

Mobile screen remote control starts in Device management.

Press the remote control icon on the paired Android row and a separate window opens, showing the Android screen on your PC. The first time you start it, you need to approve screen capture on Android, and after approval you can operate it right in the same window.

Check required permissions See related shortcuts

Controls

Operate directly in the remote control window.

  • Left click, drag, and wheel scroll are passed through to the Android screen as is.
  • Right click acts as Back, and middle click acts as Home.
  • PC keyboard input, including non-English text, is delivered to the current input field on Android.
  • If the screen does not appear, check whether the Android screen capture approval popup is in front, and if needed, stop and start again.

Permissions

The items you need to turn on are split by feature.

It is not designed to turn everything on from the start. Accessibility is the required condition for input control, and the rest are requested or recommended only when needed.

Required

Accessibility service

Required to control Android with your PC mouse and keyboard, and to handle screenshot requests and remote input.

When starting remote control

Screen capture approval

Approve it in the Android system popup when you start mobile screen remote control. Secure screens may not be shown.

When connecting

Nearby Wi-Fi devices

On Android 13 and above, it may be requested when finding a PC on the same network. It is not asked right after the app launches.

When starting the service

Notifications

Used to show the background connection status. Requested only when needed as you turn on the connection service.

When using brightness

System settings change

To adjust Android brightness with Alt + Shift + ↑ / ↓, you must allow it in Android settings the first time you use it.

Recommended

Battery limit exception

Check this only if the connection drops often after the screen turns off. It is a recommended setting that improves stability.

File Transfer

Send PC files via the transfer screen, Quick Drop zone, or PC-to-PC drag.

Choose files on the transfer screen, or drop files onto the small drop zone on the PC screen to send them to the selected Android device or Sub PC. In PC-to-PC, hold a file and drag it across the screen edge where the target PC is placed, then drop it on the target PC. To send from Android to the PC, use the Android Transfer tab or the share menu.

Quick Drop

Quick Drop zone

The drop zone position and enabled state are saved in the PC app. When it gets in the way, hide or show it again with the global shortcut.

Ctrl + Alt + D
  • File transfer uses the PC -> Android or PC -> PC path based on the selected target.
  • PC-to-PC file drag is used when the target Sub PC is connected and placed on the arrangement board.
  • If you hold a file and move it to the placed screen edge, the drag continues to the target PC, and dropping it where you want sends it.
  • If transfer does not start, check the Android/PC connection status and the selected device first.

Quick Connect

Connect instantly with the number shortcuts in the device list.

The Device management list in the PC app shows Ctrl + Alt + 1~9 shortcuts for the first nine devices. You can connect a frequently used Android device or Sub PC right away, without having to find the app window again.

  • Ctrl + Alt + 1 connects the first device in the list, and Ctrl + Alt + 2 connects the second device.
  • Press the shortcut for an already connected device once more to disconnect it.
  • When a device connects or disconnects, a small notification appears briefly at the bottom-right of the PC screen.

Auto Reconnect

Devices that reappear can reconnect automatically.

With auto reconnect on, the PC app tries to reconnect when the last device becomes ready again or when there is only one ready device. It does not show a separate notification as the mouse moves between devices.

Advanced: PC to PC

Connect another Windows PC via Settings, Device management, then placement.

Keep the PC role of the computer that starts control as Main PC in the Settings screen. Run the computer to be controlled as a Sub PC, then search for the target PC in the Main PC's Device management screen and enter the 6-digit code shown on the other PC to pair.

  • If the PC role button in the Settings screen is Main PC, you can start Android and Sub PC connections.
  • When the target PC row appears in Device management, enter the other PC's 6-digit code and press Pair.
  • After pairing, when the target PC row shows Connected, adjust the placement on the Phone position / input direction screen.
  • If you need to operate while watching the target PC screen, press the remote control icon on the connected target PC row.

Arrangement

Set the real entry direction on the arrangement screen.

  • Place the target PC on the left of your PC, and control moves to the target PC when you cross the left edge.
  • Android can stay on the same arrangement board too. When the target PC and Android are connected at once, place each one separately.
  • The sidebar connection status shows the currently selected target and entry direction. If the direction is wrong, drag it again on the arrangement board.

PC Remote Control

You can also remote control the Sub PC screen in a separate window.

  • In the Main PC's Device management, press the remote control icon on the connected Sub PC row to open the PC screen remote control window.
  • Once the window opens, you can view the target PC screen and send mouse clicks, drags, and wheel scrolls right away.
  • PC keyboard input is also delivered to the target PC. For example, shortcuts like Ctrl + C and Ctrl + V run on the target PC.

Quality

Choose quality to fit the situation and reconnect.

  • Fast prioritizes responsiveness, and Standard is tuned for general use.
  • Use High quality when you want a sharper view of the screen. The better the network, the more stable it is.
  • After changing quality, you must reconnect for the new setting to apply. Closing the window stops remote control.

PC-to-PC Screen Flow

On screen, follow this order.

The Main PC is the PC that starts the connection, and the target PC is the PC that will receive mouse and keyboard control. For the Sub PC's pairing code, enter the 6-digit number at the bottom of the screen as is.

1
Check the PC role in Settings

The side that starts control must have its PC role set to Main PC in the Settings screen. The Main PC can start Android and target PC connections.

2
Check the Sub PC's pairing code

Run OneMouse on the PC to be controlled as well, and check the 6-digit pairing code at the bottom of the sidebar. The example shows a numeric code like 101884.

3
Pair the target PC in Device management

Find the target PC row in the Main PC's Device management screen, enter the Sub PC code in the other PC 6-digit code field, then press Pair.

4
Check the Connected and Selected status

When the target PC row changes to Connected and gets a Selected badge, it is the current mouse entry target. If Android and the target PC are both shown, it acts based on the selected device.

5
Place it on Phone position / input direction

Drag the target PC rectangle to the direction it actually sits, on the left, right, top, or bottom of your PC. Android can stay on the same arrangement board too.

Keyboard Shortcuts

Global PC app shortcuts and Android control shortcuts.

Quick connect and Quick Drop shortcuts work directly on the PC. Android control shortcuts work while the mouse is on Android.

Shortcut Action Note
Ctrl + Alt + 1~9 Instantly connect one of the first nine devices in the device list. Press the number of an already connected device again to disconnect it.
Ctrl + Alt + D Hide or show the Quick Drop zone. Drop a file onto the drop zone to send it to the selected Android device or Sub PC.
Enter In a search box, runs Search/Done. Sends the Android IME's Search/Done action first, instead of a normal line break.
Shift + Enter Inserts a line break in the input field. Use it to split lines in chat or when typing long sentences.
Ctrl + A Selects all the text in the current input field. If the app does not expose the selection range directly, OneMouse helps track the selection state.
Ctrl + C / X / V Runs Copy, Cut, and Paste. Copy and Cut also sync straight to the PC clipboard when possible.
Shift + Arrow keys Extends the text selection range. Some apps may limit the Android accessibility selection action.
Alt + ↑ / ↓ Adjusts the Android media volume. It runs first even when an input field has focus, and holding it repeats the adjustment.
Alt + Shift + ↑ / ↓ Adjusts the Android screen brightness. You must allow the Android system settings change permission the first time you use it.
Print Screen Captures the Android screen and opens it in the PC's default image viewer. Uses the accessibility screenshot feature on Android 11 and above. Secure screens may not be captured.
Ctrl + Alt + S Runs the mobile screenshot instead of Print Screen. Use it when the Print Screen key is hard to use on a laptop or remote keyboard.
Alt + Tab Opens the Android recent apps screen and switches apps. Hold Alt and tap Tab repeatedly to move to the next app, and release Alt to select.
Ctrl + Alt + Backspace Forces input control back to the PC. When the cursor is stuck on the Android side, it returns to PC input and shows a mouse return notification on the PC.

Daily Use

Common flows after connecting

  • On the PC, select the Android device or Sub PC to control right now and adjust its position.
  • Handle mobile search boxes, login fields, and message composing with your PC keyboard.
  • Send PC files by choosing them on the transfer screen or dropping them on the Quick Drop zone on screen, to the selected Android device or Sub PC.
  • When sending to a Sub PC, you can also hold a file and drag it across the placed screen edge to drop it on the target PC.
  • Long-press to select text and press OneMouse to PC to send it straight to the PC clipboard.
  • If you need to keep watching and operating the mobile screen, start mobile screen remote control with the remote control icon in Device management.
  • If you only need to check one screen, take a screenshot with Print Screen or Ctrl + Alt + S and view it on the PC.

Recovery

When the cursor doesn't come back

If the cursor that moved to Android does not return to the PC, press Ctrl + Alt + Backspace on the PC. This shortcut returns mouse input to the PC, sends the return signal to Android again, then shows a return notification on the PC.

Screenshot

View mobile screenshots right on the PC.

When you capture the Android screen, it is sent to the PC and opens in the Windows default image viewer. It may fail on screens with a security policy or when Android accessibility screenshots are not allowed. Use mobile screen remote control when you keep watching and operating, and use the screenshot shortcut when you only need to check one screen.

When It Fails

What to check when it fails

  • After an app update, turn the OneMouse Accessibility service off and on again.
  • Check whether it is a capture-blocked screen, such as banking, DRM, or a secure keyboard.
  • If you captured too quickly in a row, wait a moment and try again.
  • Check that the PC and Android connection is still active.
